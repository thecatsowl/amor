Is Rurality Compatible with High-Tech Industry?

Demographics, Economy, Mass Communication and Political Culture make up the Context of Wyoming Politics.

Demographically, the minorities of the Equality State make up a very small percentage of the whole population: Afican Americans account for less than two percent; Native Americans make up another two percent; and the Hispanic population is another 9 percent.

The economy of Wyoming resembles Honduras, a Central American banana republic, whereby the state's main resource is subject to global fluctuations. Mass communication is consumed via television as opposed to the internet.

Mass Communication and Political Culture are determining factors, however, in providing a potential for a unique and robust software development culture.

It would be very difficult to gauge how much demographics, the economy, mass communication and the political culture affect quality of life, the state's art scene, personal expression and the high technology industry. In this post-COVID world, remote work and the technology that furthers it will prove ever more relevant. There is ample opportunity for a computer programmer in Sheridan to code for a company in Texas, California, New York, or anywhere in the nation. The author compares Wyoming's economy to that of a Third World Nation like Honduras; unlike Honduras, however, the Equality State has a robust academic system, a high quality government and an entrepreneurial spirit. Authors such as Annie Proulx who have written about Wyoming and lived here for extended periods of time have mentioned the high suicide rate; I feel the authors should have provided those statistics: namely, rates on suicide and indices on depression. I feel developing the arts, information technology, and a style of self-expression would broaden opportunities for Wyomingites.
